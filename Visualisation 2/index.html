Education in Australia

This visualisation provides a comprehensive overview of key educational metrics across Australia, including student attendance, student-teacher ratios, international student demographics, and school funding from 2009 to 2023. By examining these aspects, we gain insights into the allocation of resources, regional differences in educational engagement, and the evolving presence of international students. These metrics highlight how Australia's educational landscape adapts to meet both domestic needs and global interests.

Student-Teacher Ratios by State (2023)

This map illustrates student-teacher ratios across Australian states and territories in 2023. Western Australia and New South Wales show higher ratios, indicating more students per teacher, while Victoria has one of the lower ratios, suggesting more individualized attention for students. These regional variations reflect differing resource allocations in education.

Growth of International Students by State or Territory and Year (2002-2021)

International student growth showed strong positive trends until 2020, when the growth rate dropped. This decline reflects the impact of events such as the global pandemic on student mobility and enrollment.
The highest growth was 1900% in 2010, by Kuwait. Furthermore, the largest groups of international students are from China and India. These nationalities contribute significantly to the international student body in Australia, influencing both educational policies and cultural diversity on campuses.

Total School Income and Income per Student by State and Year (2009-2022)

This visualisation presents trends in total school income and income per student across Australian states from 2009 to 2022. Over this period, New South Wales and Victoria consistently lead in total income, while Northern Territory recorded the highest income per student in 2022, at $26,453. The steady increase in income per student across most states reflects ongoing investments in educational resources, varying by state to address regional needs.

Student Attendance by State/Territory and Year (2014-2023)

This visualisation highlights trends in student attendance rates across Australian states and territories from 2014 to 2023. The school attendance data for the year 2020 has not been published. This is due to inconsistencies caused by varying health guidelines and changes in schooling arrangements across Australia in response to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Attendance rates are an essential indicator of student engagement and can reflect a range of regional factors. New South Wales and Victoria consistently record some of the highest attendance rates, averaging around 93% in the earlier years, while Northern Territory shows lower rates, with a rate of 76% in 2022.

The trends over time reveal how different states and territories approach attendance initiatives and highlight potential areas for targeted support. While attendance rates in regions like Queensland and Western Australia remain stable, modest fluctuations suggest the impact of various factors. These findings emphasize the need for localized strategies to boost student engagement, especially in areas facing persistent attendance challenges, ensuring that all students benefit from consistent educational opportunities nationwide.

The data reveals regional disparities in both funding and attendance, with states like New South Wales and Victoria showing high attendance rates and substantial funding allocations. International student growth remains concentrated in a few states, driven by countries like China and India. Meanwhile, variations in student-teacher ratios and income per student suggest differing priorities and capacities across regions. Together, these insights underscore the importance of ongoing investment and targeted strategies to support equitable and effective education throughout Australia.
